Researchers create AI systems that play games like checkers for several reasons:

* **To push the boundaries of artificial intelligence:** Games like checkers provide a complex and challenging environment for AI to learn and improve in. Success in these domains demonstrates advancements in areas like search algorithms, evaluation functions, and machine learning.

* **To test and refine algorithms:** Game playing offers a controlled setting to test and compare different AI algorithms. Researchers can analyze the performance of various techniques and identify strengths and weaknesses.

* **To gain insights into human cognition:** By studying how AI systems learn to play games, researchers can gain insights into human thought processes, decision-making, and problem-solving strategies.

* **For educational purposes:** Game-playing AI can be a valuable tool for teaching students about artificial intelligence concepts and programming techniques.
